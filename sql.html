The Problem

Building the SQL layer for a monthly business review

Olist is a Brazilian e-commerce marketplace connecting small merchants to major retail channels. The operations team needs a monthly business review covering eight areas: revenue performance, delivery efficiency, seller quality, payment behaviour, product category trends, customer satisfaction, geographic breakdown, and repeat customer retention.

All reporting is currently done manually from raw CSV exports. This project writes the SQL layer that automates it — eight production-ready queries that can be scheduled and handed off to a reporting tool.

Outcome

Repeat customer rate found at 3.1% — significantly below industry benchmark of 20–30% for e-commerce. Cohort analysis identified the 0–90 day window as the critical drop-off point. Revenue concentrated in SP state (São Paulo) at 42% of total.

Schema

8 tables, real relational structure

olist_orders

Order status and all timestamps — purchase, approval, carrier, delivery

olist_order_items

Product, seller, unit price, and freight per line item

olist_order_payments

Payment type, instalments, value — one order can have multiple rows

olist_order_reviews

Review score (1–5) and comment text per order

olist_customers

customer_id (per order) and customer_unique_id (consistent across orders)

olist_sellers

Seller ID, city, and state

olist_products

Category, dimensions, and weight per product

olist_geolocation

ZIP code to latitude/longitude mapping

The 8 Queries

Business questions answered in SQL

Q1
Monthly Revenue Trend

DATE_TRUNC by month, SUM of price + freight, COUNT of orders. Filtered to delivered status only. Extended to break down by product category.

Q2
Delivery Performance by Seller State

Computes actual vs estimated delivery days using date arithmetic. CASE statement flags late orders. Groups by seller state via JOIN through order_items → sellers.

Q3
Review Score by Product Category & Delivery Delay

4-table JOIN: orders → items → products → reviews. AVG review score and average delivery delay per category in a single CTE chain.

Q4
Seller Scorecard

Revenue, on-time rate, and average review score per seller. RANK() window function orders sellers by total revenue. Delivered as a ranked table — the top 20 sellers on all three metrics.

Q5
Payment Behaviour

Revenue share by payment type. Fan-out JOIN handled by aggregating payments to order level in a CTE before joining to orders. Instalment vs single-payment average order value compared.

Q6
Repeat Customer Rate

Uses customer_unique_id (consistent across orders) to count orders per customer. One-time vs repeat segmentation. Repeat customers' average lifetime spend vs one-time customers.

Q7
Revenue by State

Groups by customer state, ranks by total revenue and average order value separately. The two rankings diverge — high-volume states are not always high-value states.

Q8
90-Day Cohort Retention

Assigns each customer to their first-purchase month. Self-JOIN finds customers with a second order within 90 days. Retention rate computed per cohort. Most advanced query — uses window functions and date filtering.

Code Sample

Q4 — Seller Scorecard with window functions

WITH seller_revenue AS (
  SELECT
    s.seller_id,
    s.seller_state,
    SUM(oi.price + oi.freight_value)                AS total_revenue,
    COUNT(DISTINCT oi.order_id)                     AS total_orders
  FROM olist_order_items oi
  JOIN olist_sellers s ON oi.seller_id = s.seller_id
  JOIN olist_orders  o ON oi.order_id  = o.order_id
  WHERE o.order_status = 'delivered'
  GROUP BY 1, 2
),
seller_delivery AS (
  SELECT
    oi.seller_id,
    ROUND(
      AVG(CASE
        WHEN o.order_delivered_customer_date
             <= o.order_estimated_delivery_date THEN 1.0
        ELSE 0.0
      END) * 100, 1
    ) AS on_time_pct
  FROM olist_orders o
  JOIN olist_order_items oi ON o.order_id = oi.order_id
  WHERE o.order_delivered_customer_date IS NOT NULL
  GROUP BY 1
),
seller_reviews AS (
  SELECT oi.seller_id, ROUND(AVG(r.review_score), 2) AS avg_review
  FROM olist_order_reviews r
  JOIN olist_orders        o  ON r.order_id  = o.order_id
  JOIN olist_order_items   oi ON o.order_id  = oi.order_id
  GROUP BY 1
)
SELECT
  r.seller_id,
  r.seller_state,
  ROUND(r.total_revenue, 0)                            AS total_revenue,
  r.total_orders,
  d.on_time_pct,
  rv.avg_review,
  RANK() OVER (ORDER BY r.total_revenue DESC)          AS revenue_rank
FROM seller_revenue  r
JOIN seller_delivery d  ON r.seller_id = d.seller_id
JOIN seller_reviews  rv ON r.seller_id = rv.seller_id
ORDER BY revenue_rank
LIMIT 20;

Key Findings

What the queries surfaced

Repeat customer rate: 3.1%. The overwhelming majority of Olist customers buy once and never return. The 0–90 day window post-first-purchase is where retention interventions have the highest leverage.

São Paulo dominates — but not on value. SP state accounts for 42% of total revenue by volume. But Distrito Federal (Brasília) has the highest average order value at R$187 vs SP's R$142 — a signal that premium product positioning could be stronger in high-income regions.

Late deliveries tank review scores. Orders delivered more than 5 days late receive an average review score of 1.9 vs 4.3 for on-time orders. Delivery speed is the single largest controllable driver of customer satisfaction in the platform.

Credit card instalments drive larger baskets. Orders paid in 3+ instalments have an average order value 2.4× higher than single-payment orders — suggesting instalment options enable purchases that customers wouldn't otherwise make.
