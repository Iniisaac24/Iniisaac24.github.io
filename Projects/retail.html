Approach

From weekly sales to a scored forecast

01

Assemble features

Joined store × department weekly sales to economic features (temperature, fuel price, CPI, unemployment, markdowns) and store metadata (type, size).

02

Baselines

Seasonal-naive (52-week lag) and Holt-Winters additive trend + yearly seasonality on the company total.

03

LightGBM

Lag features (1, 2, 4, 52 weeks) and rolling means at store-department level, scored with weighted MAPE — robust to the many near-zero department sales that break plain MAPE.

Findings

Yearly seasonality is the whole game

LightGBM wins at every level — 1.37% MAPE on the company aggregate and 7.79% weighted MAPE at store-department granularity, the level that actually drives inventory decisions.

The 52-week lag and week-of-year are the top predictors — the Thanksgiving/Christmas spike is the dominant seasonal signal, with recent-week momentum (lag_1) close behind.

The aggregate is deceptively easy. Even a seasonal-naive baseline reaches ~2% on the smooth company total — so the granular store-department task is where modelling earns its keep.
