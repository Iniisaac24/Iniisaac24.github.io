Findings

Overtime and early tenure are the danger zone

Overtime is the clearest signal: 30.5% attrition with overtime vs. 10.4% without. Combine it with under-2-years tenure and attrition hits 55.1% — more than 3× the company average.

The flight-risk model is decision-useful. A Random Forest scores each employee (AUC-ROC 0.773); flagging the top 20% highest-risk captures 55.9% of all leavers at 45% precision — a 2.82× lift over blanket outreach.

Top drivers: monthly income, age, overtime, years at company, total working years, and stock-option level — a picture of young, lower-paid, early-career staff.

Recommendations

Three interventions

  1. Cap or compensate overtime for early-tenure staff. The overtime × <2-year cohort loses over half its people — the highest-ROI place to intervene.
  2. Review early-career pay bands. The low-income young segment churns at 23.5%; a tenure bonus around 18 months targets the biggest risk cluster directly.
  3. Bring stock options forward. Low stock-option level correlates with leaving — early equity for high-potential juniors is a cheap retention lever.
