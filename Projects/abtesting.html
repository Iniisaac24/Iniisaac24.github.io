Part 1 · The A/B test

A clean negative result

GroupConversionTest
Control (old page)12.04%z = −1.31 · p = 0.19
95% CI on lift: [−0.39, +0.08] pp
Treatment (new page)11.88%
Verdict — do not ship. With a passing Sample Ratio Mismatch check (145,274 vs 145,310, p=0.95) the randomisation is trustworthy, so the result can be believed: the new page shows no significant conversion lift, and the point estimate is even slightly negative. Manufacturing a "win" here would be malpractice — the value is in calling it correctly.

Catalog over-indexes on value — 21% of purchases but 26% of revenue, i.e. larger average baskets. A budget shift toward catalog for high-value segments is worth testing next.

Linear multi-touch attribution distributes each customer's spend across channels by their purchase mix; overall campaign response rate was 15%.

Approach

Rigour before results

01

Validate the experiment

Dropped mismatched page assignments and duplicate users, then ran a Sample Ratio Mismatch check — before reading any result — to confirm the split wasn't corrupted.

02

Test properly

Two-proportion z-test with a 95% confidence interval on the lift, not just a bare p-value.

03

Attribute revenue

Linear attribution across web, catalog, and store from purchase-level data.
