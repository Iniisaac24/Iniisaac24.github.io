Approach

From price series to risk metrics

01

Pull live data

3 years of daily adjusted close for 13 holdings via yfinance, with the S&P 500 (^GSPC) as benchmark and the 13-week T-bill (^IRX) as the risk-free rate.

02

Returns & risk

Daily returns, annualised return and volatility, Sharpe ratio, 95% historical Value at Risk, and maximum drawdown.

03

Diversification

A full correlation matrix, flagging any holding pair above 0.85 as concentration risk.

04

Dashboard

An interactive Streamlit app (app.py) recomputes every metric for a chosen ticker set and lookback — one-click deployable on Streamlit Community Cloud.

Findings

Strong risk-adjusted returns, genuinely diversified

Beat the benchmark with lower volatility. The equal-weight portfolio returned 23.6% annualised vs. 20.9% for the S&P 500, at a Sharpe ratio of 1.61 — strong risk-adjusted performance.

No concentration risk. Not a single holding pair exceeds 0.85 correlation, and the average pairwise correlation is just 0.167 — the portfolio is genuinely diversified, with no cluster to unwind.

Downside is quantified and contained. A 95% 1-day VaR of 1.04% means losses stay under ~$1,039 per $100k on 95% of days; the worst peak-to-trough decline over three years was ~15%.

Metrics are as of 2026-07-07 and recompute from live market data every time the code runs.
